About Me

Welcome! I'm a PhD student in Biomedical Engineering at the Wallace H. Coulter Department at Georgia Tech and Emory University. My work focuses on developing AI-based companion diagnostics and predictive biomarkers for precision oncology in advanced prostate cancer.

Currently, I am a Doctoral Researcher working under the supervision of Dr. Anant Madabhushi. My research interests include computational pathology, deep learning for medical imaging, treatment response prediction, and precision medicine. I develop AI models that analyze multi-center phase 3 clinical trial data to identify patients who will benefit from treatment intensification versus standard-of-care therapy.

I received my Master's degree in Systems and Computing Engineering from Universidad Nacional de Colombia in 2023, where I worked on interpretable weakly-supervised learning for automatic grading of prostate cancer histopathology. My doctoral minor is in Artificial Intelligence, and I have published research in high-impact journals including Clinical Cancer Research and presented at leading conferences such as ASCO, AACR, USCAP, and AUA.

Selected Publications

A Computational Pathology Model to Predict Docetaxel Benefit in Localized High-Risk and Metastatic Prostate Cancer

Medina, S, Tokuyama, N, Hammouda, K, et al.

Clinical Cancer Research, 2025

Developed and validated AI-based predictive biomarkers for treatment selection in advanced prostate cancer across multi-center phase 3 clinical trials, achieving clinically significant risk stratification for identifying patients who benefit from docetaxel treatment intensification.

Interpretable Weakly-Supervised Learning Through Kernel Density Matrices: A Digital Pathology Use Case

Medina, S, Romero, E, Cruz-Roa, A, González, FA

PLOS ONE, 2025

Proposed an interpretable deep learning framework for automatic grading of prostate cancer histopathology using weakly-supervised learning with kernel density matrices, enabling better understanding of model decisions in clinical settings.

Dhamdhere, R, Modanwal, G, Mutha, P, Medina, S, et al.

Kidney360, 2025

Developed computational methods to extract features from fundus photographs that predict cardiovascular event risk in chronic kidney disease patients, demonstrating the potential of AI in ophthalmologic-cardiovascular risk assessment.

Salguero J, Abdeltawab H, Hammouda K, et al.

AACR Annual Meeting 2024

Developed AI methods to analyze 3D glandular network morphology in prostate tissue, identifying features associated with likelihood of biochemical recurrence after radical prostatectomy surgery.
